Hello outfit members!

As many of you have already heard, the officer team has been busy finishing up the requirements for the Lieutenant rank.

In this post, we want to let you know of the requirements to become a Lieutenant, as well as give you the chance to apply for the promotion.
To keep it simple, the requirements will be listed at the end of the post and general information will be given in this text.

The Lieutenant rank is meant to be for members with more advanced certifications that can be trusted to complete tasks reliably during squad and platoon play. This means that the attitude, as well as the general communication, must be excellent. However keep in mind that a microphone is still not required, as some people are great writers too. What is that attitude we are talking about? Following orders given by squad and platoon leads is probably the biggest part of it, however, the general behaviour will count towards it as well.

To make it easier on everybody, we would like members who unlocked the necessary certifications and are ready to be promoted to comment on this post.


The required certifications to become a Lieutenant are the following:

Required Certifications

Infantry Certifications
  • Squad Certifications:
    First Level of the Beacon certification
  • Light Assault
    Ambusher Jump Jets - Tier 1/5
  • Combat Medic
    Medical Applicator - Tier 6/6
    Nano-Regen Device - Tier 5/6
    Revive Grenade
    Flak Armor - Tier 4/5
  • Engineer
    Nano-Armor Kit - Tier 6/6
    Reserve Hardlight Barrier
  • Heavy Assault
    Nemesis VSH9 (Lock on Anti Air Rocket Launcher)
    Flak Armor - Tier 4/5
  • MAX
    Second Quasar (or any two anti-infantry weapons)

Vehicle Certifications
  • Sunderer
    Gate Shield Diffuser - Tier 2/4
    Deployment Shield - Tier 1/4
These are the certifications you need to have unlocked to become a Lieutenant. Next is the list of recommended certifications to unlock to increase overall versatility.

Thanks for the question Prometheus, it's a fair concern.

We just discussed it with the Leaderteam again, and came to the conclusion that we will stay with the required certifications.

The reasoning behind this is the following:

The Lieutnant is supposed to be able to fill in any role needed. If we (for example, since it was the class in question for you) ask for more Heavy Assaults in the squad, we expect Lieutnants to be able to switch to the Heavy Assault with reasonable certifications. Even if you do not play the class usually (and we generally won't force you to) we as leaders expect our Lieutenants to switch to classes needed instead of having other members filling the roles they do not have certed into at all.

I hope you understand our decision, if you have any further questions feel free to ask!
